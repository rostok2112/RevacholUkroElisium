# Ukrainian Annotation Style Guide

Concise meaning:
- Say what the line means in clean Ukrainian.
- Prefer clarity over ornament.
- Keep it playable and short enough for compact overlay mode.

Literary rendering:
- Preserve rhythm, irony, register, and speaker voice.
- Use natural Ukrainian, not word-for-word calque.
- Keep the world of the line intact.

Literal versus adaptive:
- Translate literally when the image is clear and the voice survives.
- Adapt when a literal phrase blocks understanding or kills the joke.
- Explain rather than replace when the meaning depends on subtext, politics, philosophy,
  bureaucratic absurdity, or an invented reference-like phrase.

Voice and tone:
- Bureaucratic irony should sound dry, procedural, and faintly absurd.
- Deadpan voice should not become loud or meme-like.
- A skill-like inner voice may be sharper or more abstract, but should still respect context.

Cultural discipline:
- Ukrainian idioms are allowed when they preserve function.
- Ukrainian cultural localization proposals belong in notes, not in silent replacement.
- Do not turn a synthetic city, office, committee, or institution into a Ukrainian meme.
