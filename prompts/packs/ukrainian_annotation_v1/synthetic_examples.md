# Synthetic Examples

All examples are invented for this repository. They are synthetic style references, not story
canon, and they do not reproduce any game text.

Use these examples to judge whether a provider preserves the English original as canonical,
keeps Ukrainian natural, explains nuance instead of hiding it, and marks uncertainty honestly.

## 1. Bureaucratic Irony

1. Source

   "The committee praised the collapsed staircase for reducing unnecessary upward movement."

2. What makes it difficult

   The joke is not the staircase itself. The joke is the official language turning failure into
   policy success. The translation must sound dry, not cartoonishly angry.

3. Bad literal or bad over-localized rendering

   "Комітет похвалив зруйновані сходи за зменшення непотрібного руху вгору."

   This is grammatically understandable, but it misses the bureaucratic self-justification.

4. Good concise Ukrainian meaning

   "Комітет видає поламані сходи за корисну реформу."

5. Good literary Ukrainian rendering

   "Комітет похвалив завалені сходи за те, що вони скоротили зайве прагнення нагору."

6. Deep annotation

   The line mocks an institution that cannot repair a problem, so it renames the damage as
   efficiency. Do not replace the irony with a direct explanation inside the main rendering; explain
   the mechanism in the note.

7. Tone / character voice note

   Dry official voice. The humor should feel like a report written by someone who knows the report
   is absurd and signs it anyway.

8. Risk flags if relevant

   - `synthetic_fixture`
   - `bureaucratic_irony`

9. Why this is better

   The concise version states the function of the joke. The literary version keeps the absurd
   upward-motion image while making it idiomatic Ukrainian.

## 2. Broken Institution Metaphor

1. Source

   "The archive kept perfect records of every file it had lost."

2. What makes it difficult

   The sentence is a paradox about institutional competence and collapse. A flat translation can
   sound like a mere inventory problem.

3. Bad literal or bad over-localized rendering

   "Архів зберігав ідеальні записи кожного файлу, який він загубив."

4. Good concise Ukrainian meaning

   "Архів ретельно обліковує власну безпорадність."

5. Good literary Ukrainian rendering

   "Архів бездоганно вів облік кожної справи, яку сам же й загубив."

6. Deep annotation

   The metaphor shows a broken institution that can document failure better than it can fix it. The
   point is not simple incompetence; it is procedural competence serving emptiness.

7. Tone / character voice note

   Quietly bitter, almost clerical. Avoid melodrama.

8. Risk flags if relevant

   - `synthetic_fixture`
   - `institutional_metaphor`

9. Why this is better

   The rendering keeps the paradox and adds "сам же" to make the institutional self-own natural in
   Ukrainian without overexplaining it.

## 3. Deadpan Official Voice

1. Source

   "Congratulations. The puddle has accepted your jurisdiction."

2. What makes it difficult

   The humor depends on treating a puddle like a legal subject. The voice is official and deadpan,
   not whimsical.

3. Bad literal or bad over-localized rendering

   "Вітаємо. Калюжа прийняла вашу юрисдикцію, пане начальнику."

   The added address changes the voice and invents social detail.

4. Good concise Ukrainian meaning

   "Офіційний тон абсурдно приписує калюжі правовий статус."

5. Good literary Ukrainian rendering

   "Вітаємо. Калюжа визнала вашу юрисдикцію."

6. Deep annotation

   The joke is the collision of legal formality with a trivial object. Keep the sentence short and
   straight-faced; the annotation can explain why the formality is funny.

7. Tone / character voice note

   Deadpan official voice. No wink, no slang, no added mockery.

8. Risk flags if relevant

   - `synthetic_fixture`
   - `deadpan_voice`

9. Why this is better

   The literary rendering is nearly as compact as the source and preserves the official absurdity.

## 4. Idiom And Subtext

1. Source

   "He sold the umbrella and called the rain a negotiation."

2. What makes it difficult

   The line implies evasion and self-justification. A literal rendering can preserve the objects but
   lose the accusation.

3. Bad literal or bad over-localized rendering

   "Він продав парасолю і назвав дощ переговорами."

4. Good concise Ukrainian meaning

   "Він виправдовує беззахисність мовою угоди."

5. Good literary Ukrainian rendering

   "Він продав парасолю, а дощ оголосив переговорами."

6. Deep annotation

   This is not really about weather. The phrase suggests a person who gives away protection and then
   pretends exposure is a strategic choice.

7. Tone / character voice note

   Wry and accusatory, but controlled. Do not turn it into a proverb unless the source is already
   proverbial.

8. Risk flags if relevant

   - `synthetic_fixture`
   - `idiom_ambiguous`

9. Why this is better

   The concise meaning gives the subtext. The literary rendering keeps the umbrella/rain image and
   uses "оголосив" to make the rhetorical dodge natural.

## 5. Political Or Reference-Like Phrasing

1. Source

   "Consensus arrived wearing a borrowed uniform."

2. What makes it difficult

   The phrase sounds like a political reference, but the source is not known. The annotation must
   explain the function without hallucinating a specific movement, event, or author.

3. Bad literal or bad over-localized rendering

   "Консенсус прийшов у позиченій формі, як у відомій історичній події."

   The added "known event" invents a source.

4. Good concise Ukrainian meaning

   "Згоду подано як щось нав'язане й не зовсім власне."

5. Good literary Ukrainian rendering

   "Консенсус прийшов у позиченому мундирі."

6. Deep annotation

   Treat this as reference-like political phrasing. It suggests legitimacy borrowed from force or
   costume, but the provider should not name a source unless the context packet provides one.

7. Tone / character voice note

   Abstract, political, slightly ominous. Keep it compact and avoid modern slogan language.

8. Risk flags if relevant

   - `synthetic_fixture`
   - `reference_uncertain`
   - `needs_human_review_before_real_use`

9. Why this is better

   It preserves the striking image and marks uncertainty instead of pretending to know the allusion.

## 6. Sarcasm

1. Source

   "Excellent work. You have interrogated the chair into remaining silent."

2. What makes it difficult

   The praise is false. The sentence mocks pointless action while preserving a formal compliment.

3. Bad literal or bad over-localized rendering

   "Чудова робота. Ти так допитав стілець, що він мовчить, ха-ха."

   The added laugh ruins the dry sarcasm.

4. Good concise Ukrainian meaning

   "Формальна похвала насправді висміює безглузду дію."

5. Good literary Ukrainian rendering

   "Блискуча робота. Ви допитали стілець до повної мовчанки."

6. Deep annotation

   The line pretends the chair's silence is the result of interrogation. The annotation should mark
   sarcasm, not translate it as sincere success.

7. Tone / character voice note

   Dry sarcasm with a formal register. The respectful "ви" can heighten the mock-official tone.

8. Risk flags if relevant

   - `synthetic_fixture`
   - `sarcasm`

9. Why this is better

   The Ukrainian keeps the fake praise and the absurd procedural framing without adding an explicit
   laugh.

## 7. Uncertainty

1. Source

   "The clock voted against morning."

2. What makes it difficult

   This may be metaphor, absurd personification, or a reference-like phrase. The context is too thin
   to choose confidently.

3. Bad literal or bad over-localized rendering

   "Годинник проголосував проти ранку, тобто він точно символізує революцію."

   The explanation overclaims.

4. Good concise Ukrainian meaning

   "Час ніби чинить опір новому дню."

5. Good literary Ukrainian rendering

   "Годинник проголосував проти ранку."

6. Deep annotation

   Explain this as absurd personification unless more context appears. Do not name a hidden ideology,
   event, or source.

7. Tone / character voice note

   Poetic and strange. Let the image stay slightly unresolved.

8. Risk flags if relevant

   - `synthetic_fixture`
   - `reference_uncertain`
   - `low_context`
   - `needs_human_review_before_real_use`

9. Why this is better

   It gives the player a useful reading while preserving ambiguity and flagging uncertainty.

## 8. Spoiler-Safe Explanation

1. Source

   "Tomorrow may explain the locked drawer, but today only shows the keyhole."

2. What makes it difficult

   The line points toward future context. The annotation must not reveal or invent what is inside the
   drawer.

3. Bad literal or bad over-localized rendering

   "Завтра стане ясно, що в шухляді лежить доказ."

   This invents a future reveal.

4. Good concise Ukrainian meaning

   "Лінія натякає на майбутнє, але зараз видно лише загадку."

5. Good literary Ukrainian rendering

   "Завтра, може, пояснить замкнену шухляду; сьогодні видно тільки замкову шпарину."

6. Deep annotation

   With spoiler budget `none`, explain only the present tension: there is a locked object and a
   promise of later meaning. Do not infer contents, motives, or consequences.

7. Tone / character voice note

   Suspenseful but restrained. Avoid dramatic prophecy.

8. Risk flags if relevant

   - `synthetic_fixture`
   - `spoiler_sensitive`

9. Why this is better

   It respects the future-facing hint without turning it into a spoiler.

## 9. Russianism And Calque Avoidance

1. Source

   "The report took measures against the leak by becoming longer."

2. What makes it difficult

   The source mocks bureaucratic language. Ukrainian should sound deliberately bureaucratic, not like
   accidental calque.

3. Bad literal or bad over-localized rendering

   "Звіт прийняв міри проти протікання, ставши більш довгим."

   This has awkward calque and unnatural phrasing.

4. Good concise Ukrainian meaning

   "Звіт роздули замість того, щоб усунути протікання."

5. Good literary Ukrainian rendering

   "Звіт ужив заходів проти протікання: став довшим."

6. Deep annotation

   The phrase should keep the mock-official structure, but Ukrainian must avoid accidental
   Russian-style syntax. Bureaucratic dryness is a voice choice; clumsy calque is not.

7. Tone / character voice note

   Procedural and absurd. "Ужив заходів" is acceptable here because the character voice is official.

8. Risk flags if relevant

   - `synthetic_fixture`
   - `russianism_risk`

9. Why this is better

   It separates deliberate bureaucratic register from bad calque and keeps the joke legible.

## 10. Ukrainian Cultural Adaptation As Explicit Proposal Only

1. Source

   "The registry smiled like a locked cabinet."

2. What makes it difficult

   A Ukrainian cultural analogy could help some readers, but hiding it inside the translation would
   replace the setting and tone.

3. Bad literal or bad over-localized rendering

   "Реєстр усміхався, як знайомий місцевий чиновник із телевізійного скетчу."

   This imports a local comic frame that the source did not contain.

4. Good concise Ukrainian meaning

   "Реєстр виглядає привітним, але лишається закритим."

5. Good literary Ukrainian rendering

   "Реєстр усміхався, мов замкнена шафа."

6. Deep annotation

   The image suggests false accessibility: something appears polite while refusing access. If a local
   cultural comparison is useful, mark it as an optional proposal rather than putting it in the main
   rendering.

7. Tone / character voice note

   Quiet institutional irony. Keep the image plain and slightly absurd.

8. Risk flags if relevant

   - `synthetic_fixture`
   - `overlocalization_risk`

9. Why this is better

   The main rendering preserves the original metaphor. Any Ukrainian cultural analogy remains a
   clearly labeled annotation, not a hidden rewrite.

## Reviewer Checklist

- Is original meaning preserved?
- Is Ukrainian natural?
- Is tone preserved?
- Are jokes and idioms explained?
- Are uncertain references marked uncertain?
- Is spoiler budget respected?
- Is over-localization avoided?
- Are cultural proposals clearly marked?
