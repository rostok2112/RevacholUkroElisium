# Product Ideology

## The product is a companion, not a replacement

A full Ukrainian replacement patch sounds attractive, but it can silently destroy nuance if the translation is weak. Disco Elysium's value is not just "what happened"; it is voice, rhythm, class irony, political absurdity, inner-skill schizophrenia, and the exact placement of lines in dialogue trees.

Therefore the product should:

- keep English original available,
- offer Ukrainian comprehension instantly,
- reveal deep context only on demand,
- remember translation choices,
- evaluate itself continuously,
- allow human override for important lines.

## Layers of meaning

Every visible line can have four layers:

1. **Original** — exact English from the game.
2. **Ukrainian translation** — literary but faithful.
3. **Quick annotation** — "what is going on here" in one short sentence.
4. **Deep annotation** — idiom, sarcasm, politics, lore, skill voice, local joke, cultural echo.

## Ukrainian cultural adaptation policy

Use Ukrainian idioms and memes only when they reproduce the *function* of the original:
tone, register, class position, absurdity, insult force, or bureaucratic texture.

Do not turn Revachol into Ukraine. Add Ukrainian resonance as an optional annotation or alternative phrasing unless the line clearly benefits from localized idiom.
